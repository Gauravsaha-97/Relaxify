FAQs about Importance of Breathing during Meditation

-> Can I meditate just by breathing?

Yes definitely! But it is not compulsary. In the focus-based and mindfulness meditation, you don’t need to regulate your breath in any way. You just need to pay attention to it and be mindful of the process.

-> What should be out focus during meditation?

Primary focus should be on breathing and calming your mind. It tends to be very effective to calm the mind, achieve a state of tranquillity.

-> How to do breath meditation to build mindfulness?

Firstly, Start by finding a quiet comfortable place with minimum distractions.Rest your hands on your thighs, palms facing downward or upward or on top of each other, in front of your abdomen, whichever you feel most comfortable.With a proper sitting posture, you can practice longer and prevent drowsiness at the same time. That’s why it’s important to keep your back naturally straight.

-> Why do so many meditation methods focus on the breath?

There are some good reasons why so many meditation methods focus on the breath. As a meditation object, breath has some advantageous qualities. Breath is readily available. You don’t need to buy it or find it. It’s always with you. Breath is neutral.

-> What kind of experience does meditation give you?

Meditation gives you an experience, an inner state, where what is you and what is yours is separated. There is a little distance, there is a little space between what is you and what you have accumulated. For now we can understand this as meditation.
